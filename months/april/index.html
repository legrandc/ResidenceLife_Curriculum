April | Pass the Torch

After having spent a year on your floor, help residents reflect on all that they have gained from their residential community and ways that they can give back to that community, to Winthrop, and to the community at large through civic engagement and servant leadership. It’s time to pass the torch!

The learning outcomes met by this month’s bulletin board, program, and resident interactions are:

  • Academic Excellence - Residential students will actively engage in a learning experience outside of the classroom at least once per month.

  • Self-Development - Residential students will be able to identify their values and understand how they impact their choices and actions.

Program – You should complete an educational program where you become an expert on resiliency and/or you host a community expert. If you are an ASC RA, you must connect your program to your ASC.

Bulletin Board - Your bulletin board should incorporate at least 2 approved resources (not in QR code form). If you are an ASC RA, you must connect your bulletin board to your ASC.

  • Go door to door asking residents for food donations for Pilgrims Inn or Project Hope, while sharing information with them about local poverty and service opportunities.
  • Invite someone from Career and Civic Engagement to discuss service opportunities in the area and how they can benefit residents personally and professionally.
  • Invite a former Peace Corps Volunteer or AmeriCorps VISTA to share their experience in serving on a larger scale.
  • Host a fun social event where the entry fee is toiletries or food to be donated to a local shelter.
  • Arrange for your residents to write letters to soldiers overseas, the elderly in a local nursing home, or to prisoners through a pen pal program.
  • Work with your residents to make breakfast or lunch for your custodial staff.
